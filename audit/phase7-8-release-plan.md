## Risk Matrix

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|--------|------------|
| ProGuard breaks serialization | Medium | High | S4.4 release testing. Current rules are comprehensive but Gson + KotlinX Serialization dual usage adds risk. |
| Cleartext HTTP in production | **High** | **Critical** | **S1.1 is MUST-FIX before any release.** Currently ALL HTTP traffic is allowed. |
| Crashlytics traces unreadable | High | High | S1.4 mapping upload. Without it, crash reports are useless. |
| Player crash on specific codecs | Medium | High | FFmpeg + MPV fallback exists. S2.2 performance traces will help diagnose. |
| Room migration data loss | Low | Critical | S1.7 migration tests. Migrations exist but are untested. |
| Play Store rejection | Medium | Medium | S4.1-S4.5 compliance checklist. Privacy policy is the most common rejection reason. |
| Accessibility complaints | Low | Medium | S3.1-S3.2 audit. Not a launch blocker for TV apps but important for quality. |

**Bottom Line**: PlexHubTV has a solid architecture and good Firebase integration. The critical blockers for production release are: (1) network security config allowing cleartext HTTP, (2) missing privacy policy, (3) hardcoded strings, and (4) Crashlytics mapping upload. These are all fixable in Sprint 1. The app can realistically ship to Google Play in 4-6 weeks with focused effort on this plan.
